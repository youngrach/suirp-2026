[blog] looking forward

20 May 2026

in just over a week, I leave for the UK. it doesn't feel real yet. I've never been to Europe, and now I'm about to spend two whole months there on my own. exciting, yes -- but also quite scary. but the good kind of scary, I think.

my main concerns for when I get there:

  • making it from Edinburgh -> Newcastle-upon-Tyne -> my housing once I land (with more planning, this should be fine)
  • getting up to speed and finding my place in the lab
  • feeling homesick and lonely
this is my first time solo-travelling. at a certain point, I'll need to embrace these feelings and just let myself feel them. the only (healthy) way out is through, as they say.

so yes, the anxiety is a thing. but also, so much could go right this summer. this is my last summer as an undergrad, and probably the last time I'll get paid to spend months abroad. I really want to soak it in and enjoy the moments as they unfold. some things I want to do while there:

  • read books written by English authors (see my reading list on the home page)
  • practice taking pictures with my newly acquired camera
  • connect with the research world in the UK and learn something new
  • don't be afraid to ask basic questions or feel stupid -- thought ≠ truth
  • explore the local culture (food, slang, lifestyle, perspectives)
  • collect some British phrases to bring back home

grateful for this new adventure!
