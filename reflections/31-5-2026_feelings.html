feelings

31 May 2026

we often yearn for things from the past (well, at least i do). anything from a past friendship or a shirt that's now too hole-y to wear with dignity in public, to a favorite mini spoon left behind at a hotel or an almond croissant.

but really, it's not the things that make a difference. it's how we felt while we had them. and that's honestly a wonderful realization because things are all (to a certain extent) external entities, while our feelings spout from deeply internal sources. so even if something no longer belongs to us, the feelings which accompanied that thing can remain ours forever... as long as we remember and cherish them. the feelings live on in our memories of the things, and in many cases grow and develop with repeated remembrance over time (similar to a well-behaved curry).

all in all, this is a very comforting thought for me. yes, life is ephemeral, nothing lasts forever, etc., but as long as we remember the experience of a thing, the essence of that thing (at least the parts of it relevant to us) is kept alive and well. all the cherished things we yearn for -- all the almond croissants and pre-school zebra shirts and old teddy bears -- the important parts of them are nurtured and carried within us.

we simply need to remember.
